# Engagement: Arvore — AWS Cost Comparison & Migration Assessment

## Context

Brazilian EdTech company (likely digital reading/content platform). No AE on the deal — direct SA engagement. Customer came to us with their AWS bill asking for a cost comparison and migration plan. Monthly AWS spend: $13,534 (Feb 2026 actuals).

Infrastructure profile: mature stack — EKS, RDS Multi-AZ, Redshift, Airflow (MWAA), Kinesis, EMR, S3 (~87 TB), CloudFront, Datadog ($2,584/mo). Mix of x86 and ARM Graviton instances (c7g, m6g, r6g, t4g).

No SA-to-AE coordination — pure technical motion.

## What we did

1. Parsed their AWS billing CSV (Feb 2026, ~900 KB, 3000+ line items) to extract service-level cost breakdown
2. Built a line-item cost comparison: AWS actuals vs DO estimated equivalents
3. Ran a technical qualification scoring (61/100 — moderate fit)
4. Identified migration risks and a phased approach (DB + ECR first, compute later)

No POC was initiated. Engagement ended at the qualification / cost-comparison stage.

## Results

- **Projected savings on DO: $5,341/mo / $64,092/yr (39%)**
- Fit score: **61/100** (moderate — analytics stack gap, no São Paulo region, ARM fleet, RI lock-in risk)
- Customer received cost comparison + qualification deliverables
- **Lost:** Customer received aggressive discounts from their incumbent (presumed AWS or another cloud)

## What worked

- Parsing the raw AWS billing CSV directly surfaced service-level cost drivers without needing the customer to self-report. The $393/mo ECR and $263/mo VPC/NAT line items were surprises to us — not things a customer would volunteer.
- RDS → DO Managed DB was the strongest value story: 60% cheaper for equivalent specs, identical wire protocol (PostgreSQL), zero-downtime migration path. This is the reliably good DO argument.
- Leading with VPC/NAT elimination ($212/mo, goes to $0 on DO) is a concrete, easy-to-understand win that resonates with technical buyers.

## What didn't work

- **The 39% savings story lost to a discount.** When a customer self-initiates a cost comparison, they're almost certainly already in a negotiation with their current provider. Our cost comparison gave them leverage to extract a better deal from AWS — they didn't need to actually migrate. This is a known dynamic; consider how to position DO's qualitative advantages (simpler ops, predictable billing, no egress surprise bills) earlier so the conversation isn't purely about price.
- **No AE involvement.** Without a commercial relationship, there's no backstop when the customer walks away. A technical motion that ends at a deliverable without a commercial champion rarely converts.
- **We never got past qualification.** The discovery questions (São Paulo region, RI expiry, m5.16xlarge workload identity) were never answered. Unknown RI commitments may have been the real lock-in, not the discount.

## What to do differently next time

- When a customer comes in with their AWS bill, ask **before** building the comparison: "Are you currently in a renewal or negotiation with AWS?" If yes, your deliverable becomes their negotiation ammo, not your sales tool.
- Push to get discovery questions answered in the same session as the cost comparison — don't let the engagement end at a deck.
- Qualify the São Paulo region requirement on first contact for Brazilian customers. If they need SP and DO doesn't have it, that's a pass, not a qualification.

## For the next SA who sees this customer type

**Brazilian company, cost-driven, shares their AWS bill unprompted:**

High chance they're already in a discount negotiation with AWS. Build the comparison fast (use the CSV playbook in `skills/cost-models/`), but use it to open a discovery conversation — don't hand it over without getting the discovery questions answered first. The DO value story for this profile is RDS → Managed DB (60% savings, zero migration risk) + NAT Gateway elimination + ECR cleanup. Lead with those three and get to a POC on DB migration before they take the deliverable to their AWS rep.

If they have ARM instances (c7g/m6g/r6g/t4g) and no São Paulo requirement confirmed, those are two immediate qualification flags — address both before investing further.
